Unusual shape: **no act structure**. It is a *plateau*, not an arc. The creator trades
catharsis for **uninterrupted arousal** — correct for short-form feed, where average
watch-time matters more than emotional payoff.

## Mechanism-by-mechanism (WHY the brain enjoys it)
| Driver | In the video | Neuro/psych explanation |
|---|---|---|
| **Anticipation** | ult cast → "Ultimate"/"Iron Body" banner before a kill | Dopamine fires on *prediction of reward*, not reward itself — the banner is the cue |
| **Tension** | losing-score clips (10v12, 6v10), low-HP fights | Uncertainty raises arousal; ambiguous outcome holds attention |
| **Relief** | "Kill"/"Immune" resolves the fight | Tension→release cycle = the core pleasure loop |
| **Dopamine loop** | reward every ~9 s | Variable-ratio-ish schedule (each clip slightly different) = slot-machine stickiness |
| **Competence display** | 1v multi survivals, *Immune* dodges, high KDA | Vicarious mastery; mirror-neuron "I could be this good" |
| **Status signaling** | rising kill counts, MVP-grade plays | Viewer borrows the player's status by watching/sharing |
| **Power fantasy** | tank wading in, surviving (Iron Body), killing | Wish-fulfillment of dominance & invulnerability |
| **Validation** | the montage *is* a flex; comments will praise | Parasocial reciprocity → engagement |

## Why each is psychologically load-bearing
- **Variable reward >> fixed reward.** Because clips come from *different matches*, each
  payoff is novel (different enemies, angles, scores). Novelty re-opens the dopamine gate
  every ~9 s — a fixed, predictable highlight would habituate fast.
- **Invisible cuts protect the fantasy.** A visible "compilation" cut would remind the
  brain it's edited (reduces the "one unstoppable run" illusion). Seamlessness sustains
  *flow-state spectating*.
- **HUD = scoreboard = social proof.** The brain reads the live kill count as objective
  evidence of skill, which licenses sharing ("look how cracked this is").
- **Silence at the end = withdrawal.** Removing audio + image abruptly creates a small
  dissonance the viewer resolves by *acting* (follow/replay).

## Target audience psyche
MLBB players (esp. Thai/SEA) who (a) aspire to rank up, (b) enjoy vicarious dominance,
(c) seek quick arousal between queues. The content sells **identity** ("I main this
hero / I want plays like this"), not information.

## One-line thesis
> The video is a **variable-ratio dopamine machine disguised as a single heroic run**,
> where authenticity (HUD, real-speed, native VFX) is the trust layer that lets the
> power-fantasy land.
