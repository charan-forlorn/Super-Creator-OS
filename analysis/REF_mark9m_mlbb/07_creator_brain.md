# 07 · Creator Brain Reconstruction (Phase 6)

Written in the creator's first person, each decision backed by video evidence.

## My goal
"I want a 2-minute clip that makes me look like an unstoppable [hero] main and converts
strangers into followers. Watch-time first, follow second."

## Decision: open cold on a calm setup frame, not a kill
- **WHY chosen:** I need the first second to read instantly as "strong MLBB player". A
  calm frame + the beat dropping at 0.7 s lets the *music* do the hook while the eye
  orients. (Evidence: 0:00 idle river frame, music jumps −33→−15 dB at 0.7 s.)
- **WHY alternatives rejected:** Opening mid-explosion is visually unreadable in <1 s
  (too much VFX, no anchor). A title card wastes the hook. Cold-open wins.

## Decision: stitch 6–9 different matches but hide every cut
- **WHY chosen:** No single match gives me enough back-to-back highlights. Pulling the
  best 9 s from many matches concentrates value. (Evidence: timer 02:16→08:17→10:22→
  14:47→13:18→17:21→08:39 across the runtime; scores all differ.)
- **WHY hidden:** If the cuts were visible, it reads as "edited compilation" and breaks
  the "one god run" fantasy. Same map + same HUD + cutting on the kill-flash makes seams
  invisible. (Evidence: scene-score never exceeds 0.27.)
- **WHY this placement:** I cut *on the brightest VFX frame* (e.g., the kill flash at
  ~0:52) so the eye is saturated and can't register the seam.

## Decision: punch-in on fights, never speed-ramp
- **WHY chosen:** Mobile action is tiny; the zoom forces the viewer's eye onto my kill.
  Real speed proves the mechanics are legit. (Evidence: HUD larger/cropped at 0:48/0:50/
  0:90; constant 30fps, no ramp artifacts.)
- **WHY alternatives rejected:** Slow-mo would scream "I'm hyping a mediocre play". I want
  "no tricks, just skill".

## Decision: add zero editor VFX
- **WHY chosen:** MLBB's own banners ("Kill", "Immune", "Ultimate") and damage numbers
  are already perfect dopamine cues and they're *credible* (part of the game). My own
  flashes would bury them and look amateur. (Evidence: only in-game UI present.)

## Decision: keep the full HUD, especially the score
- **WHY chosen:** The live kill score is a free progress bar and proof I'm winning. It
  turns the whole video into one open loop. (Evidence: score visibly climbs 5→23 within
  clips; HUD never cropped out.)

## Decision: one continuous ~80–85 BPM track, beat-1 on frame-1
- **WHY chosen:** Music is the glue that carries energy across my hidden cuts — if the
  audio never stops, the video never feels like it stops. (Evidence: unbroken bed 0.7→
  115.7 s.)
- **WHY half-time/phonk:** Matches the heavy, dominant "power" mood; SEA gameplay-edit
  meta favors it.

## Decision: hard-stop to a silent black end card with my @handle
- **WHY chosen:** I spent 2 minutes building parasocial goodwill; the abrupt silence is
  my close — it makes them notice the handle and decide. (Evidence: 3 s dead-air + search
  bar `@mark9m_`.)
- **WHY no loop seam:** I'd rather convert a follow than farm a replay; the black frame is
  a deliberate "the show's over, come find me".

## My implicit rule-set (what I optimize on)
1. Best 8–11 s per match, nothing slower.
2. Every clip must contain a kill/standout or it's cut.
3. Never let two boring seconds touch each other.
4. Keep it real-speed, real-HUD, real-VFX = credibility.
5. Music never stops until the CTA.
6. Make 9 matches look like 1.

## What I am NOT doing (and why it matters for cloning me)
- No storytelling, no commentary, no facecam, no captions/text edits, no transitions FX.
  My value is **curation + concealment + pacing**, not production polish.
